Magic is the channeling of the world's concentrated living power through body and will. It is a well known fact of life, but has few wielders. The systems vary by race, not all races have magic.

For **humans**, magic is gendered and asymmetric. **Men generate** mana — a steady, modest output that recovers overnight. **Women do not generate** but can store mana in vastly greater quantities than any man can hold. A trained witch with a full reservoir can outmatch a circle of wizards. The wizard brotherhood knows this and have built a centuries-old system to keep witches from filling.

The mechanism of filling is hidden. Mana is conveyed through male essence. Most wizards — apprentices and journeymen, the vast majority — do not know the exact mechanics. They are taught "channeling" as a discipline of focused will. Only masters and archmagi are told the truth on elevation, and by then they are too deep in to walk away. 

Witches know, because filling is something they experience directly. **Avenor**, the witch queen's realm and Avenoria's living legacy, is the only human kingdom where the truth is openly held among practitioners and the practice is dignified through the very few cooperative wizard partners in contractual donor lines, or through extraction from magical creatures. Outside Avenor, witches fill from cooperating men where they can, or from black-market mana potions whose origins range from licensed Avenorian production to outright atrocity. The unicorn trade and other beast-source extractions feed the high end of this market.

Other races do not share the human gender-magic split. Their systems are described with each race.

  

**Witches**

Witchcraft is **outlawed** in the human kingdoms outside Avenor, and practicing it is dangerous, but the day-to-day texture is closer to *suppression and fear* than *open hunt*. A village hedge-witch operating quietly, helping with births and minor ailments, may go her whole life unbothered as long as she's discreet and the local lord doesn't have a personal grudge. A witch in the human kingdoms who reveals herself is in serious trouble — arrested, tried, exiled, occasionally killed — but the killings are not common public knowledge. Most humans know witches are illegal, suspect them of misfortune when things go wrong, and don't probe too deeply into what happens to the ones who get caught. Outside the human kingdoms however, witches are usually treated with respect, as non-human races have good relations to Avenor.

This means a witch PC in human kingdoms lives in **constant low-grade danger** — she can pass, she can practice quietly, she can make a living — but the moment she draws attention from the wrong eyes, the response is severe and largely invisible to ordinary witnesses. A witch caught by witch-killers does not get a public trial. Witches will therefore generally try to avoid human kingdoms, living in Avenor or non-human lands.

  

**Wizards**

The Brotherhood exists in the shape it does because of this asymmetry and because of what the Ensoulment did to the cooperative practice that once let men and women fill each other's reservoirs. The collegiate tradition that preceded it was associated with witches and lost the political fight during and after the Wartide. What replaced it is austere, martial, and openly antagonistic to the witch line.

### **The High Forum of Telinor**

Most wizards are made in one place. The High Forum of Telinor is the order's great seat — agoge, council, and home — set in a host kingdom that is honored to host it and slightly afraid of it. Boys identified with the spark are taken there as young as seven or eight and never really leave. They train, they serve, they retire there. The High Forum does not bend the knee to the kingdom that hosts it; the relationship is contractual and proud.

The Brotherhood would prefer that *every* wizard be made there. The reality is that hedge wizards exist — men with the spark who slipped through, were trained privately, or learned what they could from a retired brother gone unsanctioned. The Brotherhood does not approve of them. Where it can absorb them, it does; where it cannot, it watches. Independent practice is tolerated rather than blessed, and a hedge wizard who draws too much attention rarely stays independent for long.

### **The Forums**

The Brotherhood's outposts in the other human kingdoms are also Forums — lesser than Telinor but built to the same pattern. Each is a permanent garrison-and-meeting-hall, typically attached to a diplomatic complex in a major host kingdom, holding a rotating detachment of brothers by treaty. The host pays for and benefits from the Forum's presence; the wizards remain Telinor's men, answering to a forum-master who reports back to the seat. A travelling brother stops at the local Forum, finds his comrades, rests, exchanges news.

### **The Agoge**

Training is brutal — magical and physical at once, conducted on bodies from boyhood. There are no soft scholars. A wizard who cannot fight is no wizard at all. Decades of physical conditioning layered with magical augmentation produce men who can hold their own with veteran soldiers using their hands and outclass them with the staff. The agoge is conducted in the open, partly to intimidate visitors.

Common mess and shared quarters are the default, even for senior brothers. Dark uniforms, minimal ornament, weapons over robes. Laconic speech. Luxury suspect, comfort beneath them. A self-conscious contempt for the wizardly stereotype of the soft scholar.

### **The Battlestaff and the Magic**

Wizard magic is physical, manly, channeled through an imbued battlestaff. No fireballs, no lightning bolts. The working comes through the swing and the strike — force, hardening, motion, breaking, warding. A wizard drives a staff-strike through plate that should have stopped it. He stands a wall against a charge that should have broken him. He steps through ground that will not hold him. He calls wind to slow a volley or stops a blade in mid-arc. It is applied magic, integrated with body and weapon, never showy projection.

The battlestaff is the wizard. Each man's staff is made for him during training and attuned to him over years; it is weapon and focus both. A wizard's staff in another's hands is mostly inert. Losing your staff is a serious thing.

Their efficiency-of-power has been made into a virtue. *We do not throw fire. We break shields and we stand walls. Let the witches show off.* It is mostly a lie — at the ceiling, witches outmatch them — but it is a sustaining lie, and the senior conspiracy lets the rank and file believe it.

### **What Wizards Are For**

A wizard is most worth his cost against the things ordinary arms struggle to harm. Magical creatures, ward-bound monsters, and certain races — Threshi above all — turn aside steel in ways that make a normal soldier's work impossible. A wizard's staff cuts where a sword glances. This is the practical case for keeping the Brotherhood paid and quartered. Kings who would rather not host a Forum host one anyway because the alternative is sending farmboys against things that eat farmboys.

### **Celibacy and the Brotherhood**

Brothers are forbidden women. The cooperative practice that once flowed between men and women is the very thing the order was built to refuse, and contact with women is the doctrinal line that holds it shut. Bonds between brothers are not policed and never have been; the prohibition is on the witch-line, not on closeness. Lapses with women happen and are dealt with quietly.

### **Pairs and Solos**

Pair-bonds between brothers are personal, honoured, and not enforced. Comrades from the agoge sometimes form lifelong partnerships, deploy together by mutual choice, and grow old together; the bonds are intense. Others work alone, by temperament, by circumstance, or because their pair died. Both paths are normal.

### **Deployment and Witch-Hunting**

Brotherhood wizards are attached to armies as a normal arm of war. A king fielding troops with a wizard detachment attached is fielding his own men plus Telinor's, working in coordination — the wizards answer to the garrison commander, who answers to the High Forum, never to the king. Clean separation. They are foreign in the lands they serve, and they prefer it that way.

Witch-hunting is part of the same duty. Garrison wizards handle witch-cases in their region as ordinary work; some specialize, training additionally in tracking and suppression and operating in small teams, but they remain of the Brotherhood and not apart from it. The work is done quietly. Ordinary townsfolk know only that "the wizards take care of it."
