# Hedoria — Regions and Locations Reference

A reference document for the world of Voyage. Covers the eleven mapped regions of the continent, their spatial relationships to each other, and the known locations within each. Race, faction, and political detail belong in separate documents — this one stays on terrain, geography, and what stands where.

  

## **The Continent at a Glance**

The continent is laid out on a coordinate grid where higher y values are north and higher x values are east. The settled and known portions of the world span three columns and four rows:

The human kingdoms hold the periphery: Avenor on the south coast, Udorath on the western Whitestone coast, Nadorim on Lake Wildering, and Kelmar in the Hinderance Highlands at the foot of the great northern mountains. The interior — the central forest, the high ranges, the open steppes, and the deep desert plateau — is held by the lifted and wartide races.

## **Spatial Relationships**

The continent's interior is dominated by **The Reach**, the great central forest, which sits directly at the heart of the mapped world. To its north lies the **Long Steppe**, open grassland that opens further west into the **Deep Steppes**, the wide Hofnar interior. North of the Long Steppe, on the east side, the country rises into the **Hinderance Highlands**, the rough cold high country at the foot of the great mountains; further north still, beyond the highlands, lies the **Sun-Scoured Plateau**, the deep desert.

  

To the east of The Reach the country breaks against the **Shieldcap Mountains**, a wild high range that runs north–south. Where the southern arm of those mountains meets the sea, the terrain becomes the **Impasse** — near-impassable cliff-and-ravine country that cuts Avenor's peninsula off from any direct overland route to the eastern coast.

  

South of The Reach lies **Avenor**, the river-plain that holds the city-state of the same name. Avenor sits on the south coast. To Avenor's west, separated by mixed country and the southern reach of the mountains, runs the **Whitestone Coast**, the western coastal region. North of the Whitestone Coast, and west of The Reach, lies **Lake Wildering**, the great inland lake.

  

Travel across the continent follows a few main corridors. From Avenor a road runs north into The Reach and onward toward the Long Steppe. From the Whitestone Coast a road runs north and east to Lake Wildering, and from the lake's northern end a route runs northeast across the steppes toward the Hinderance Highlands. The Shieldcap Mountains separate the western and central kingdoms from whatever lies east of them, with only a handful of passes traversable in season. The Sun-Scoured Plateau sits beyond the Hinderance Highlands and is reached only by long traverse through hard country.

  

## **The Regions**

### **Avenor**

A wide low river-plain bisected by a slow broad river fed by smaller tributaries running down from the mountains to the north. The land is among the most fertile on the continent: deep loam, long arcs of grain country, vineyards on the lower slopes, orchards along the watercourses, and grazing meadows on the higher ground. Wetlands and reed-marshes line the river's lower reach where it meets the sea. The coastline runs in long sand and shingle beaches broken by tidal estuaries. Inland, the plain rises gradually into rolling foothills before breaking against the mountains at the northern edge.

  

**Locations in Avenor:**

  

  - **Avenor (city)** — the city-state itself, a great city of white marble rising from the river-plain, organized around three vast plazas and crossed by nine bridges. Sub-areas include the Palace District, the Queen's College, the Plaza of the Queen, the Plaza of the Open Hand, and the Plaza of the Listening Stones.
  - **Valhelde** — a large old town closely tied to Avenor, notable for its great bridge across the Orteliol river.
  - **Red Harvest** — a crossroads community with several inns and taverns and a handful of tradesfolk catering to travellers.
  - **Riverwatch** — a strategic river outpost on the outskirts, designed to monitor trade traffic. Includes a Customs Pier and a Command Tower.
  - **High-Garden Hamlet** — a picturesque agricultural village on a low ridge, known for its terraced flower fields and medicinal herbs. Includes the Flower Terraces and a Village Commons.
  - **Echoing Hollow** — a deep wooded valley with ancient ruins beneath heavy moss and canopy. Includes a Sunken Sanctum and an Overgrown Path.
  - **Cinder-Reach Outpost** — a rugged mining camp on the stony edges of the territory, extracting ore from the cliffs. Includes a Smelter and a Mine Entrance.
  - **Iron-Core Consortium Deep-Gallery** — a sprawling subterranean mining complex run by the Iron-Core Consortium under contract. Includes an Ore Processing Floor and an Overseer's Office.
  - Minor villages and hamlets
  - Creature dens and old ruins

### **The Reach**

A vast forest of layered character. The margins are open woodland and forest-edge country — scattered birch and oak, brush, fern-heavy clearings where the canopy thins and sunlight reaches the floor. The interior is deep old-growth: closed canopy, damp ground, moss-thick trunks, shadowed valleys where the light never reaches midday strength. River-valleys thread between, cutting wide grass-lined corridors through the trees. Seasonal change is dramatic — lush green in spring and summer, russet and gold through autumn, sparse and dark in winter when the bracken dies back. Game is plentiful: deer, boar, the larger forest predators, and the great elk of the river meadows. The Fernwarg hold the open and edge country; the Raknid hold the deep old-growth.

  

*Hidden:* Pre-Wartide ruins stand in some of the deeper valleys, mostly overgrown.

  

**Locations in The Reach:**

  

  - **Fernwarg village** — a small secluded settlement nestled within the dense undergrowth.
  - **Raknid treetop village** — a settlement built up among the canopy of the deep old-growth.
  - **Molvar Mound-Tunnels** — earthen mounds at the forest margins concealing intricate Molvar tunnel networks.
  - some villages and hamlets
  - Bandit camps, clusters of monsters
  - Creature dens and old ruins

### **Hinderance Highlands**

Rough high country at the foot of the great northern mountains. The land is broken and uneven — rocky grassland giving way to bare scree slopes, cut by deep gullies, narrow ravines, and sudden cliff-faces where the underlying stone breaks through. Wind is constant and the weather is hard year-round. Winters are long and severe, with deep snow that lingers into late spring. Summers are short, bright, and cold at night. Tarns and small lakes sit in glacier-cut hollows. Heather, stiff grass, and stunted thorn cover the lower slopes; bare stone and lichen the higher. The country grades upward into the mountains proper. Travel is slow on foot and worse by wagon. Inhabited by the human kingdom of Kelmar, with its capital at Shield's End on the lower steppe.

  

**Locations:** 

\- the Kelmari capital

\- High Forum of Telinor

\- border villages and hamlets

  - Bandit camps, clusters of monsters
  - Creature dens and old ruins

  

### **The Sun-Scoured Plateau**

A harsh broken desert of deep sandstone canyons and towering flat-topped plateaus. The stone runs in rust, copper, ochre, and deep gold tones that catch the late-afternoon sun and glow. Days are searing; nights are bitter cold. Water is scarce and where it is known it is fiercely held — seasonal washes at the canyon floors, hidden seeps in the cliffs, the rare deep pool in shadowed defiles. Vegetation is sparse: thorn-scrub, hardy succulents, the occasional ancient juniper clinging to a ledge. Wind has shaped the rock into spires, arches, and slot canyons that cut deep into the plateau. Travel follows old paths along the canyon floors and the high mesa tops; there are no roads. Inhabited by the Draklid in their cliff-cut clan-holds.

  

**Locations on the Plateau:**

  

  - **Draklid Clan-Holds** — a network of sandstone cliff-cut holds carved into the orange stone. Includes Clan Halls and a Sun-Gate Entry.
  - some villages and hamlets
  - Bandit camps, clusters of monsters
  - Creature dens and old ruins

  

### **Lake Wildering**

A great inland lake dominating the region, with a long irregular shoreline of bays, headlands, and reed-fringed shallows. The water is deep and cold at the center and shelves into wide marshy flats at the margins. Smaller lakes and bog-pools dot the surrounding country, connected by slow streams and stretches of fen. Mixed forest of birch, alder, and pine ranges across the dry ground between the wetlands; meadows open where the soil drains better. Waterfowl are abundant. Lake-fish, eel, and freshwater shellfish fill the shallows. Wild country runs hard against the settled shore — much of the back-country is unclaimed in any practical sense and difficult to patrol. Inhabited by the human kingdom of Nadorim, with its capital at the north end of the lake and smaller towns and villages strung along the shoreline.

  

**Locations in Lake Wildering:**

  

  - Nadorim capital
  - **Prospector's Rest** — a rocky outpost run by the Stone-Singer Syndicate, specializing in shoring up unstable veins and reclaiming abandoned claims. Includes a Shoring Wharf and a tavern called the Grinder's.
  - some villages and hamlets
  - Bandit camps, clusters of monsters

### **Whitestone Coast**

A mixed coastal region named for the chalk cliffs that face the sea along long stretches of the shore. Above the cliffs lie open chalk uplands — short grass, sheep-cropped turf, hawthorn windbreaks, and the occasional standing stone. Below them the shoreline runs in alternating cliffs, shingle beaches, and tidal flats. Inland the country drops into wide coastal wetlands: salt-marsh near the sea, freshwater fen further in, threaded by slow rivers and old drainage channels. Surviving farmland sits on the fen-edges and the chalk uplands. Reed-beds and bog-iron pans run through the marsh interior. At low tide the foundations of flooded villages remain visible along sections of the coast, the relics of dike-works lost in the Wartide. Inhabited by the human kingdom of Udorath.

  

**Locations on the Whitestone Coast:**

  

  - **Cliffrend** — the capital of Udorath.
  - **Whitestone Coastal Vein** — a cliff-side mining operation overlooking the salt-sprayed rocks. Includes a Damp Tunnel that floods at high tide and a Foreman's Outpost on the cliff edge.
  - some villages and hamlets
  - Bandit camps, clusters of monsters
  - Creature dens and old ruins

### **Shieldcap Mountains**

A wild high mountain range. Sheer ridges, broken passes, ice-bound peaks, glacier valleys, and weather that turns hard without warning even in summer. The lower slopes are heavy pine forest and scree; the middle ranges are bare rock and snowfields; the heights are stone and ice. Streams run fast and cold out of every valley. A handful of passes are travelled in season by those with means and guides; most of the range is left alone. Wild predators range the upper passes — great wolves, snow-bears, raptors, and worse. The foothills hold scattered Vorok pit-works under contract with the surrounding kingdoms. The high country is otherwise unsettled.

  

*Hidden:* Old structures stand in the high country — pre-Wartide ruins, abandoned watch-posts, and at least one site that prospectors will not enter for any contract price.

  

**Locations in the Shieldcap Mountains:**

  

  - **The Winterspring** — a cold lake high in the mountains, home to cold-blooded fish and a number of harsh-climate minor monsters.
  - **Leador's Pass — west entry, main path, and east entry** — three discrete points along a route through the range.
  - sparse hamlets
  - Bandit camps, clusters of monsters
  - Creature dens and old ruins

### **Impasse**

A near-impassable region where the southern arm of the mountains falls into the sea. The terrain is a tangled mess of steep cliff-faces, sea-cut ravines, fractured bedrock, and storm-beaten coastline. Salt spray reaches far inland. There are no roads and no clear passes. A few sheltered coves and defensible upland pockets hold extremely remote villages, trading rarely with the world outside. Wild predators range the rocks and the high broken country. Smugglers know tracks the kingdoms do not. Ruins, some pre-Wartide, stand in the deeper ravines.

  

*Hidden:* Not all of the ruins are empty.

  

**Locations:** None catalogued at this layer yet.

### **Long Steppe**

Open grassland running wide and flat under a tall sky. The landscape is rolling rather than featureless — long shallow swells, broad shallow valleys, scattered stands of wind-bent trees in the hollows where water collects. Grass runs from knee-high in the wet spring to shoulder-high by late summer, browning and laying down through autumn into the brown stubble of winter. Streams are infrequent but reliable, fed from the high country to the east, running in cut channels across the plain. Seasonal flowers wash colour across the country in spring. Wind is constant. Game runs to grouse, hare, antelope, and the larger steppe predators. Hofnar wagon-cycles cross the region on long seasonal routes, with established encampment sites along the way.

  

**Locations on the Long Steppe:**

  

  - **Deep-Galleries Citadel** — a sprawling subterranean hub built by the Iron-Core Consortium to manage primary mining interests. Serves as a central administrative and family-quarters facility for Vorok contract labor on long-term operations. Includes a Contract Hall and a Breeding Compound (housing the families and offspring of contracted Vorok workers).

### **Deep Steppes**

Vast open grassland reaching into the deep interior, wider and wilder than the Long Steppe to the east. The country runs flat to the horizon in most directions, broken only by shallow river valleys and the occasional rocky outcrop. Grass is dense and tall through the warm months, cured to standing hay by autumn, buried deep under snow in winter. Great wild herds — aurochs and proto-horse-folk known as the Far-Herds — range the deep interior in vast moving columns. Wolves and the larger steppe cats follow them. In the summer months the great Hofnar wagon-cities encamp here in formation, hundreds of vast wagons drawn up in long avenues; in winter the wagon-cities have moved south and the country is left to the herds and the weather.

  

**Locations:** None catalogued at this layer yet.
