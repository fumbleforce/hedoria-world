## **The Wartide and the Ensoulment**

The Wartide and the Ensoulment are the founding events of modern Hedoria — the catastrophe that nearly ended civilization on the continent and the working that saved it. Centuries ago, within living memory of the longest-lived races but well past the lives of any human, the first witch queen performed the great working that broke the Wartide and remade the moral architecture of the world. The continent of peoples that exists today is the consequence of that single act. So is every grievance the races still hold against one another.

### **The Wartide**

The great evil was a confederation of dark powers, varied in form and origin, united by appetite. It came to Hedoria as wave after wave — monstrous armies, corrupted lands, plague-tides, the fall of human kingdoms one after another. Every civilization on the continent faced destruction.

The Wartide had a structure, but its essential nature was overwhelming numerical and material force, not subtle corruption. It killed everything in its path. It took territory and held it. It produced more of itself faster than its enemies could kill it. It was winning because it could afford to lose ten of itself for every one of its enemies, and it had ten thousand to spare.

Conventional resistance failed. The wizard organisations of the era could not match its rate of attrition. The pre-existing non-human races could not coordinate or contribute meaningfully. The Primes held their mountain monasteries but could not project force outward. Humans, the dominant race, were dying.

### **The Working**

Avenoria's response was not redemption-magic. It was mass force-creation. She gathered every ounce of mana available to her — every cooperating wizard and the stockpiles of the wizard institutions she could reach — and used it to lift everything she could touch into people who could fight. Forests, herds, predators of the wild, animals on the edge of the killing-fields, the dark forces themselves where she could reach them.

This was not done for the newly ensouled's own sake. The places and creatures that became peoples were lifted because Avenoria needed soldiers. The fact that the lifted were now people with their own claims and futures was a consequence of the working, not its purpose. Avenor's official history frames the Ensoulment as gift and salvation. The historical record of what was happening when Avenoria did it is unambiguous: there was a war on, she was losing it, and she did this because she needed an army.

The working reached the Wartide's own hosts as well. Some of them broke under it — saw the war from the other side, saw what they could be, and fought their kin alongside the freshly ensouled. Others were caught only partway, neither cleanly redeemed nor fully Wartide, and fled the battlefield in the chaos. Both lineages survived. They are called the wartide races now, and they occupy an uncomfortable place in Hedoria — they fought beside the others in the high tide and have never quite been forgiven for what they came from.

### **Victory**

The Wartide was broken, but not destroyed. The newly-ensouled races, the wartide races that had turned, the surviving humans — eventually combined to break the Wartide's back. The great hosts were destroyed. But fragments remained. The Hollows are where those fragments concentrated, and they are still active. Many smaller Wartide creatures fled and bred in the wild places. The evil is broken but not gone.

### **The Aftermath**

This is the political core of Hedoria. The war was won. The ensouled races existed. They could not be un-ensouled. They had to live somewhere.

Humanity emerged from the Wartide scattered and weakened. Cities had fallen, kingdoms had collapsed, populations had been halved or worse, and the hereditary nobility had been decimated. The human realms that exist today are reconstructions, built in the decades after the war by survivors. Their pre-war territory was mostly gone — much of it overrun by the Wartide and now Hollows or wilderness, much held by the new races during and after the war, much simply depopulated and never reclaimed.

The new races took the lands they had fought on, and the lands no humans were left to hold. Forests, steppes, coastal waters, broken country, frontier hills — claimed by the peoples who had defended them, kept by the peoples who had bled for them. From their perspective this was the fair reward for fighting the war. They had earned their territory in blood, defending what humans could not, and Avenoria's working had created them with nowhere else to go.

From the human perspective this was catastrophic loss. Half the continent had been theirs before the Wartide. After the war, much of what they recovered they had to recover from their former allies, who claimed the land as compensation. Most humans alive today do not think about the Wartide much — it was centuries ago. What they think about is the lost territory, the wars their grandparents fought against the new races, the kingdoms that no longer exist. The Wartide is distant history. The soul-wars are recent grievance. And the figure most blame for all of it is Avenoria.

### **Avenoria Contested**

She is not, in human memory outside Avenor, a saviour. She is the woman who saved humanity by giving away its world. The standard human criticism runs: she performed the working without consulting the human kingdoms who were her allies; she lifted races into being without knowing what they would become or what they would claim; she failed to defend humanity's claims when those new peoples demanded land in compensation; she is responsible for every soul-war fought since. She saved humanity's lives at the cost of humanity's world.

The defence runs: without her, no one would be alive to have this conversation. The Wartide would have killed everything on the continent. The lifted races earned their lands by defending them. The post-war settlement was rough but inevitable — armies that win wars do not give back the territory they bled for. Humans' bitterness is the bitterness of survival, displaced onto the woman who made survival possible.

Both arguments are partly true. Hedoria has been arguing them for centuries and shows no sign of resolving them. A person's view of Avenoria tells you almost everything about their politics.

The wizard organisations carry their own grievance, narrower and more bitter. Their precursors lost authority in the working — Avenoria drew on their mana, superseded their leadership, performed a working their institutions could not have replicated, and left them permanently diminished afterward. Their official position is that her working was hubris, that conventional wizardry could have eventually turned the Wartide back without the Ensoulment, that her panic-decision created problems that have outlasted the war it solved. This is the founding grievance of the wizard institutions, and it shapes much of their behaviour to this day.

### **The Soul-Wars**

Two wars have been fought over the post-Wartide settlement, neither decisive enough to reverse it.

The First Soul-War came perhaps fifty years after the Wartide ended, when the reconstructed human kingdoms tried to retake the great forests they had lost. They failed. The forests have been held by their defenders since.

The Second Soul-War came about a century later, fought between humans and the steppe peoples over the eastern grasslands. It was inconclusive, but the new races retained most of what they held. Relations between the steppe peoples and the human kingdoms have been wary ever since.

There has been no third soul-war. The pressure for one has been building for a generation. Most of those alive today expect to see it.
