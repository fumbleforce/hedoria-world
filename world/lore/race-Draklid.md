## **The Draklid**

The Draklid are the drake-descended people of the high desert and broken plateau — fierce, long-lived, and organized into small sovereign clan-holds that no outside power has ever successfully unified or subjugated. They are not an empire. They have never wanted to be. Each Draklid clan is its own proud nation, holding its territory and its honor with a ferocity that has made them, individually, some of the most dangerous people on the continent, and made them collectively ungovernable. Other races deal with them one clan at a time. This is by Draklid design.

### **Origin and Form**

The Draklid were lifted in the Ensoulment from the drakes of the interior plateaus — the lesser kin of the great dragons, cold-blooded, predatory, patient hunters of broken country and thermal stone. They emerged into humanoid form without losing what they came from. The body remembers what it was.

An adult Draklid is tall and lean, sinewy rather than bulky, with the long-limbed build of a creature evolved for hot open country and explosive short-range pursuit. They are narrow at the waist and broad through the shoulders, with smooth muscle running through the arms and legs in the efficient, unshowy way of predators rather than the displayed musculature of combatants. Their tails are long and powerful, trailing behind them and used constantly — for balance on uneven stone, for emphasis in speech, for threat display when something needs to be made clear. A Draklid whose tail goes still and flat is a Draklid who has made a decision.

Their scales run in the warm tones of desert and cliff: rust red, copper, ochre, deep gold, sand, dark bronze, and occasionally a stark black edged with bronze that certain lineages carry and are proud of. Along the flanks and limbs, the scales pattern into markings that are specific to each clan — readable to any Draklid who knows how to look, invisible to most outsiders. These markings are not decorative. They are identification, lineage record, and territorial claim written on the body.

Their faces carry the long narrow muzzle of a drake, closer in profile to a wolf's lines than a lizard's flat bluntness — forward-set, built for tracking, with slit-pupil eyes in amber, yellow, or deep red that do not miss movement. From the brow and temples sweeps a fan of curving horns, the size and configuration of which vary by lineage and are another form of legible identity among their own kind. Their hands are long-fingered with hooked claws; their feet are clawed and digitigrade, built for hot stone and quiet approach.

Folded against their backs are the vestigial wings. They are small, leathery, fringed with scales along the leading edge, and entirely useless for flight. They have not been useful for flight in longer than Draklid oral history can reach. But they are not gone, and they are not ignored. The wings are part of how Draklid read each other: spread wide, they are display and threat; held rigid, they signal challenge; lifted slightly, attention or alertness. In ceremony they are adorned — thin jewelry worked directly into the membrane, gold wire and small stones set into the leather with practiced hands. Among the clans, wing size and shape are markers of bloodline in the same way facial markings are, and a Draklid with notably large wings carries an ancestral pride in them, however useless they may be for leaving the ground.

Some lineages have nearly lost the wings entirely — a fold of skin and a suggestion of bone at the shoulder is all that remains. This is not regarded with shame exactly, but it is noted. The clans with larger wings remember this about the clans without them.

### **The Clan-Holds**

The Draklid do not build in the lowlands. Their settlements are found in high desert, rocky plateau, and the broken country between the ranges — cliff-cut fortresses carved directly from red sandstone, terraced into the rock face over generations, with inner courtyards open to the sky and outer walls that are simply the stone of the mountain shaped. A Draklid clan-hold from a distance looks like the cliff itself has organized. Up close it is a labyrinth of carved passages, sun-warmed terraces, painted facades in rust and gold, and banners hanging in the late-afternoon heat.

The architecture reflects the culture. There are no wide avenues or great public squares of the kind human cities build. There are defended approaches, narrow corridors, overlapping sightlines, chambers with single entrances. A Draklid hold is designed by people who have always understood that the place you live is the place you may have to defend. The great hall of a clan-lord is not built to impress visitors with its size. It is built so that the clan-lord controls every approach to it.

Each hold is the domain of a single clan, led by a matriarch or patriarch whose authority is absolute within their territory and meaningless outside it. Confederations between clans exist — alliances of blood, oath, and mutual interest that can field combined forces when something is worth the cooperation — but the Draklid have never produced a king, and the suggestion that they should is reliably received as an insult. The great powers of Hedoria have made this mistake more than once. They do not typically make it twice.

### **Drake Nature and the Cold Intelligence**

The drakes from which the Draklid descend were not warm-blooded hunters. They were patient ones — stalking, calculating, conserving energy until the moment presented itself, then moving with absolute economy. The Draklid did not lose this when they were ensouled. They organized it.

A Draklid in negotiation is thinking in longer timeframes than whoever is sitting across from them. They are not in a hurry. They are aware of things that were said two meetings ago. They notice inconsistency and file it away without remarking on it. They are not friendly in the manner of races who use warmth as social lubricant, but they are not cold in any way that could be mistaken for stupidity or disinterest. A Draklid paying close attention looks, to inexperienced outsiders, like a Draklid doing nothing at all. This is a consistent source of miscalculation.

They are long-lived, significantly more so than humans and modestly more than the Hofnar — old enough that individual Draklid carry personal memories of events that human kingdoms know only as history. A clan matriarch in her fifth century has watched the rise and fall of several dynasties and has opinions about what tends to cause each. She does not share these opinions freely. When she does share them, they are accurate.

### **Beyond the Hold**

The clan structure produces many Draklid who leave. Not all of them are failures or exiles — many are simply younger children of proud lineages who must make their own name rather than remain under a parent's authority, or warriors whose skills have outgrown the scale of what their home territory can offer. These Draklid travel into the wider world of Hedoria and take work as mercenaries, bodyguards, duelists for hire, and oath-bound retainers to foreign powers willing to pay for the absolute reliability that a Draklid oath provides.

A Draklid abroad is recognizable: tall and still in the fashion of their kind, slit-eyed and formally polite, dressed in layered desert silks that look extravagant in northern cities, heavy gold at the throat and wrists and the clan sigil worked in metal at the belt. They carry their home clan's honor with them the way other races carry their name. They are not warm to strangers, but they are scrupulously fair, and in places where reliability is rare and expensive, they are sought after.

The reputation travels ahead of them. You will get what you paid for. You will not get more. And if you are unwise enough to cheat them, the consequences will be measured and final and entirely your own fault.
