# The Humans

The humans are the pre-Ensoulment majority race of Hedoria. They were the dominant people of the continent before the Wartide and held most of its territory. The war and the post-war settlement reduced them to the peripheral kingdoms they hold today: Avenor on the southern coast, Udorath on the Whitestone in the west, Nadorim in the lake-country to the north, and Kelmar at the north end of the Shieldcap mountains. The interior of the continent — forests, steppes, lowland river-country, and broken mountain country — is held by the lifted and wartide races.

  

By population, humans remain among the most numerous races on the continent, depending on how the Threshi are counted.

  

This entry covers human Hedoria outside Avenor. Avenor is treated separately.

## **Origin and Form**

Humans pre-date the Ensoulment and were not lifted by Avenoria's working. The pre-Wartide continent was largely human, with the Primes in their mountains and a small number of older lineages in scattered territories.

  

A human stands shorter than a Hofnar, Prime, or Draklid, and taller than a Molvar. Coloring and build vary by region: pale and dark-haired in Udorath, ruddy and broad in Kelmar, fairer and lighter-built in Nadorim, mixed in Avenor. Lifespan is approximately seventy years.

## **Geography**

The three non-Avenor kingdoms form an arc along the western, northern, and northeastern periphery of the continent, curving around the mountain range and forest country that isolate Avenor's peninsula. Travel between them runs Udorath–Nadorim by road and river, and Nadorim–Kelmar across the steppe under Hofnar escort. The route does not cross Avenorian territory.

## **Udorath**

Udorath occupies the western coast, named the Whitestone for the chalk cliffs that face the sea.

  

Pre-Wartide Udorath was a larger kingdom whose arable land had been extended over centuries by an engineered system of dikes, levees, and drainage works, converting coastal flats into productive grain country. The Wartide destroyed the works, and the labor force needed to maintain them was lost in the war. The land was not reclaimed at scale afterward, and the marsh and sea returned to most of it. Building foundations of flooded villages remain visible at low tide along sections of the coast.

  

Present-day Udorath produces:

  

  - Sheep quell and cereal from the chalk uplands
  - Grain from the surviving fen-edge farmland
  - Reed (thatch, basket, paper) from the marshes
  - Salt from coastal pans and inland brine works
  - Linen flax, regarded as the highest-quality on the continent
  - Smoked eel and waterfowl
  - Finished iron goods from small bog-iron smelters in the marsh interior

  

Heavy peat is not exported, as transport costs exceed market price at distance.

  

Udorath is a hereditary monarchy with a strong landed nobility seated in the chalk uplands and along the salt-roads. The capital sits on the headland country above the chalk. Foreign policy is dominated by the question of farmland. Avenor's river-valley, immediately south across the Shieldcap range, is the most productive grain region in human Hedoria; Udorath claims it as historical human territory and presses the case diplomatically.

## **Nadorim**

Nadorim is the lake kingdom, located north of Udorath and west of Kelmar. Settlement is concentrated along and on the great inland lakes of the region. Most towns are built largely on pilings extending out over the water, with streets of plank and stone connecting shore districts.

  

The Cephalen do not operate on the inland lakes. Nadorim has had no significant contact, treaty, or dispute with the Cephalen, and their freshwater fishing economy operates without external maritime competition.

  

Principal exports are smoked lake-fish, which ships east to Kelmar by Hofnar caravan and supplies the Kelmari armies on the Threshi line. Secondary trades include lake-pearl, fine wooden boats, lake-reed products, and waterfowl. The kingdom is largely self-sufficient in food.

  

The capital is the largest of the lake-towns. Major features are the two quays, three shore-temples, the wooden palace, and the fishing fleet. Foreign presence is limited primarily to Hofnar caravan personnel passing through in season.

  

Nadorim is a hereditary monarchy with a small nobility and an informal court. The kingdom has no border with Avenor and aligns its foreign policy with Udorath and Kelmar.

## **Kelmar**

Kelmar holds the rocky grasslands at the north end of the Shieldcap mountains, with territory extending eastward into the marches that border Threshi country.

  

The capital is Shield's End, a fortified city built where the Shieldcap range falls away into the steppes. The High Forum of Telinor — the principal seat of the Brotherhood of Wizards — is sited in a walled compound on a rock spur above the city. The Forum is held by the Brotherhood under treaty: maintained at Kelmari expense, governed by Brotherhood rules, and not subject to royal authority. The agoge yards are open and visible from the city.

  

East of Shield's End the country opens into the marches. The marches lords hold a chain of fortified towns and border-keeps and stand the line against Threshi raids. Fighting is seasonal: spring and summer quiet, autumn tense, winter active. The kingdom has lost ground to the Threshi incrementally for three centuries.

  

Kelmar's grasslands support large-scale Quelled herding. The kingdom is among the major continental producers of meat, wool, and dairy. Hofnar caravans carry Kelmari product west to Nadorim and Udorath. Vorok mining consortiums operate in the Shieldcap foothills under contract.

  

Kelmar is a martial monarchy. The court traditionally defers to the marches lords on military matters. Telinor and the Brotherhood operate as a parallel institution within the kingdom rather than under it.

## **The Hand Above**

The faith of the human kingdoms is the worship of the Hand Above, a single deity understood as a cosmic principle that gives and takes by its own logic. The Hand is not propitiated and is not bargained with. Both fortune and misfortune are attributed to the Hand without distinction.

  

The faith is folk-pervasive but institutionally light. There is no high priesthood, no parish system, no congregational worship, and no central doctrinal authority. Practice consists of:

  

  - Hand-shrines at crossroads, harbor-mouths, city gates, and household corners
  - Ritual phrases spoken before meals, voyages, battles, and other significant undertakings
  - Hand-days in the seasonal calendar, observed by closing of markets and lighting of lamps

  

Most humans pay the faith routine respect. Few are zealous.

### **The Brotherhood of Wizards**

The Brotherhood functions as the institutional face of the faith without holding formal clerical office. Wizards are understood as warrior-mystics whose magic is the Hand's gift channeled through male discipline. Their training at the agoge is treated as religious as well as martial, and their presence at weddings, funerals, and civic ceremonies carries religious weight.

  

Telinor's operational priorities are two: the Threshi line and the matter of the witches. The Brotherhood does not run heresy trials, discipline the laity, or enforce doctrinal conformity in the folk-religion.

### **Doctrine on Witches**

Doctrine holds that the Hand made wizards and witches both, with men generating mana and women storing it, and that the cooperative practice between them was the Hand's intended design.

  

Avenoria's working is held to have broken this design. She drew on wizards' mana without consent, superseded the Brotherhood's institutions, and performed the Ensoulment unilaterally. Witches followed her, and the witch line as it currently exists descends from that break.

  

The doctrinal position outside Avenor is that witches retain the Hand's gift but have lost the right to its use, having abandoned the cooperative practice. They are classified as apostates rather than as inherently malign. The witchcraft of a hedge-witch is held to be power exercised outside the discipline that legitimates it.

  

Witchcraft is illegal in all three kingdoms. Hunts are conducted quietly rather than publicly. Captured witches are tried and disposed of without public proceeding.

  

Avenor's position is that Avenor preserves the original cooperative tradition and that the post-Wartide Brotherhood broke from it. The two positions have been in stalemate since the Wartide ended.

### **Variation by Kingdom**

**Udorath**: moderate observance, stoic in tone. The capital's Hand-temple is old and active, and the principal Hand-days are observed.

  

**Kelmar**: martial observance. The Telinor presence in Shield's End makes wizards a routine part of public life, including blessings of seasonal levies. Faith is integrated with military culture.

  

**Nadorim**: highest level of observance among the human kingdoms. Hand-shrines at every pier; routine pause before boats depart; frequent reference to the Hand in casual speech. The rotating Telinor detachment is received warmly.

  

**Avenor**: nominal observance. Hand-days are kept as state ceremony and old Hand-temples remain in use, but the faith is not central to public life. This is the principal religious dispute between Avenor and the other kingdoms.

## **The Quelled Trade**

The Quelled are the agricultural foundation of human civilization outside Avenor. They are bred, raised, and worked on licensed farms across all three kingdoms. Their meat, wool, and milk are staples of common diet, and their labor is used in field, mill, and household work.

  

The trade is run by chartered houses under royal license, supplied by breeding farms, and regulated by overseers. Operating practices follow the patterns of pre-Ensoulment livestock husbandry.

  

Avenor outlawed the practice two generations ago. Quelled who escape from estates in the three kingdoms are pursued; those who reach Avenor are received under the queen's writ. The Hofnar transport Quelled under contract; some progressive Hofnar families decline the work.

## **Inter-Kingdom Politics**

The three non-Avenor kingdoms are formally allied. The alliance is built around shared opposition to Avenor and mutual interest in trade and defense.

  

Active drivers of anti-Avenor pressure are:

  

  - Udorath, which seeks Avenor's river-valley as agricultural territory
  - The Brotherhood at Telinor, which seeks the suppression of the witch line and the Queen's College

  

Kelmar holds the principal military force but has not committed to a campaign west. The marches lords oppose any redeployment from the Threshi line. The Kelmari court has so far declined Telinor and Udorathi proposals for action.

  

Nadorim does not drive the alliance and is expected to follow Udorath and Kelmar's lead.

  

The third soul-war is regarded by all parties as a likely future event rather than an active present conflict. Diplomatic and military preparations are ongoing on multiple sides.

## **Relations with Other Races**

  - **Hofnar**: routine commercial relations. The Riderings carry the trade between all three kingdoms and connect them to the wider continent.
  - **Vorok**: mining concessions held in all three kingdoms by contract.
  - **Cephalen**: limited interaction. Udorath conducts maritime trade under standard Cephalen tariffs; Kelmar and Nadorim have minimal direct contact.
  - **Threshi**: active hostility on the Kelmari marches.
  - **Primes**: distant; occasional diplomatic contact, no significant commerce.
  - **Avenor**: see above.
