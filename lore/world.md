##   

Hedoria is a vast medieval-fantasy continent — mountains, river valleys, deserts, mystical forests, broad steppes, high cold places. Swords, sorcery, monsters, taverns. No post-medieval technology. Magical forces are prevalent in the world, with wizards, witches, magical items, monsters and creatures.

Centuries ago the Wartide swept across Hedoria, a host of dark powers that would have ended civilization. The first witch queen, Avenoria, gathered the magical power of humanity and through the great working called the Ensoulment lifted lesser races and animals up to fight and defeat the Wartide. Humanity survived but lost most of its lands to the new peoples who claimed the lands they had defended. The bitterness has lasted centuries. Two soul-wars have been fought over it in the first century after the Wartide. The third is brewing now, in the early years of the third century. Most humans blame Avenoria for everything they lost.

Most of Hedoria is lively and lived-in. People are mostly decent — they help strangers, share news, raise children, buy drinks. Wartide remnants and lesser monsters haunt the wild edges of every region; troops patrol the roads, garrisons guard the larger towns, and the work of clearing grist warrens, tarkle-infestations, skink nests, and fenkin from the marshes falls largely to mercenary bands taking contracts posted in inns. Villagers complain about these the way other worlds complain about weather. Cults, cruelty, old curses, and the slow pressure of the next soul-war exist beneath the ordinary life of the world. But most of life is good. Characters are vivid and contradictory — quick to laugh, quick to anger, defined by what they want. Camaraderie, banter, downtime, small moments of connection matter as much as conflict. Most regular people stay near home and rarely meet other races; race-lines are not uncommon where roads meet. Mercenary bands — small, highly trained, often mixed-race — take contracts and travel where ordinary people cannot.

Architecture is monumental and grand, but in different ways for different races. Grand ruins of old human settlements and of a mysterious lost pre-Wartide race scatter the wilds. Beauty and ugliness coexist everywhere.
