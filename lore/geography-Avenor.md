## **Avenor**

Avenor is the oldest continuous city of human Hedoria — a city-state of white marble rising from a wide river-plain. It is the seat of the witch queen, the heart of human magical learning, and the only kingdom where witchcraft is practiced openly and lawfully. To its admirers, Avenor is the surviving fragment of human greatness from before the Wartide. To its detractors among the other human kingdoms, it is the realm that betrayed humanity when the other races were ensouled, broke with its kind, and harbors the founder's line still.

### **The City**

Avenor's old heart is a great city of fluted columns, vaulted halls, and broad colonnaded avenues. The central districts are organized around three vast plazas: the Plaza of the Queen before the palace, the Plaza of the Open Hand at the commercial heart, and the Plaza of the Listening Stones at the entrance to the great archive. Aqueducts older than living memory still carry mountain water to the public fountains and witch-academies. Whole quarters are paved in pale stone polished by generations of feet.

The architecture is restrained where other human capitals are gaudy. Avenor's grandeur is in proportion, in light, in the long vistas — not in gilt. Even the witch queen's palace sits within the city's geometry rather than above it. The throne hall is reached by stairs wide enough for ten abreast, worn shallow by centuries of use.

Newer districts ring the old core in concentric layers — narrower streets, taller buildings of stone and timber, foreign quarters where each people maintains its own architecture in miniature, and great mixed neighborhoods where Avenorians of every race live and work together in ways no other human city permits. The river crossing the city is wide and slow, spanned by nine bridges. The oldest, the Witch's Span, is said to have been built by Avenoria herself and is closed to ordinary traffic, used only by the queen and her household for ceremony.

Out toward the crowded outskirts, Avenor has its shadowed corners. The queen's writ runs thinner there. The night watch moves only in pairs. The realm's many refugees have made lives in those streets that the central court does not always wish to look at directly.

### **Government**

Avenor is a hereditary monarchy ruled by the witch queen, descended in unbroken line from the founder. The queen rules with the advice of the Inner Council — senior witches, ministers, and noble advisors who meet daily beside the throne hall. Below them sits the Open Court, a larger body of nobles, guild-masters, foreign envoys, and licensed witches who meet in formal session four times each year to confirm edicts, hear petitions, and conduct the realm's public business.

The queen's authority is absolute in theory and constrained in practice. A queen who ignores her council finds her edicts slow to enact, her appointments quietly resisted, her revenues mysteriously delayed. The unwritten understanding is that the queen rules and the council shapes. The realm runs best when neither pretends otherwise.

Local justice is handled by magistrates trained in Avenorian law — a body of statute and precedent among the most sophisticated in human Hedoria, drawn on by foreign courts and studied across the continent. The law is famous for its handling of magical cases, its early abolition of the husbandry trade, and its protection of foreign residents under the queen's writ.

### **The Queen's College**

Avenor hosts a single academy for witchcraft, the Queen's College — among the premier schools of magic in the realm, and uniquely in Hedoria a place where male and female practitioners study under the same roof. The college is small, fewer than a hundred students at any time, the great majority of them witches. The few male students are the consort-line wizards who serve as ritual partners and learn the cooperative practice the Brotherhood of Wizards has refused for centuries.

Witch training is a long apprenticeship — typically twelve years from enrollment to investiture. Students live at the college, study under master-witches, and are paired with consort-wizards for the ritual exchanges by which Avenorian witches fill their reservoirs. The system is dignified, contractual, and well-regarded within the realm. Outside it, the college's existence is the single fact that the rest of human Hedoria finds most intolerable about Avenor.

The college accepts students of other races, and has done so for over a century.

### **Population and Daily Life**

Avenor is a city of perhaps a million within its walls and the immediate surrounding districts. Roughly four-fifths of the population is human; the remaining fifth is mixed across every race that maintains relations with the realm. The realm's law recognizes no distinction between human Avenorians and those of other races within its territory — a fact genuinely unusual among human polities, and one of the deepest cultural differences between Avenor and the rest of human Hedoria.

The city is wealthy by any standard. Avenorian craftsmanship — textiles, enchanted goods, medicines, scholarly publications — is sought across the continent. Daily life moves to the rhythms of the great public clocks. Markets open at dawn and close at dusk; academies hold morning and afternoon sessions; public baths run through the heat of the day; wine-houses and theaters fill the evening hours.

The realm's borders are open to refugees of every race. They are processed through formal reception and integrated as citizens — a policy the rest of human Hedoria cites as evidence of Avenor's foreign character.

### **The Work of Avenor**

Avenor's primary concern is keeping the races from each other's throats, every day, in dozens of places, against constant pressure. The third soul-war is brewing. The southern human kingdoms are escalating against their neighbors. The wizard organizations openly war-talk against Avenor itself. Trade networks are fracturing in three directions at once. Avenor's diplomatic corps runs interventions, mediations, back-channels, and emergency missions across the continent without pause. The work is exhausting, mostly thankless, and the only thing keeping the visible crisis from breaking open.

The realm's second great drain is internal. Avenor's factions — the Restorationists, the Deepening, others — are not merely intellectual currents. They are organized opponents who have penetrated the court at every level. There are Restorationist witches in the queen's own household. There are Deepening sympathizers in the diplomatic corps. Intelligence is sometimes leaked. Envoys are sometimes sabotaged by their own colleagues. The witch queen knows. She is fighting it. She is not winning.

### **The Queen**

The current witch queen — Sephilia, seventh of her line to bear the title — was, until recently, exactly the ruler Avenor needed. Sharp, principled, exhausting herself at the diplomatic work, beloved by her court, trusted by every foreign power that mattered.

Something has happened to her. Her bearing is the same, her speech patterns the same, her humor with old friends still surfacing in flashes. But her decisions for the last few years have been wrong in ways her court cannot quite name and cannot quite stop. Edicts that punish factions she had previously protected. Sudden withdrawals of trust from advisors of decades. Diplomatic shifts that hurt Avenor's most important alliances. Old friends who raise the issue are met with confusion, then irritation, then — for the unlucky few — exile. Some of those exiles saw most clearly what was happening.

The exile of her daughter broke the silence within the court. The princess was the obvious heir, beloved in her own right, magically gifted, in the work of preparing for the role. Her exile last year was framed as a matter of grave dishonor that must not be discussed — some terrible act, details sealed, punishment merciful. The truth, known to perhaps a dozen people in the realm, is that the princess understood what was happening to her mother and moved to act on it, and was exiled before she could. The faction that has hold of the queen could not allow the heir to remain at court.

The princess is now somewhere in the world, formally stripped of her standing — a young witch of the royal line, powerful by bloodline but with little training, who knows the most important political secret in Hedoria and cannot prove it.

### **Avenor in the Present**

Senior witches of the Inner Council are visibly tired. The court has lost beloved figures to exile, retirement, and death. The question of succession is no longer discussed openly. Older Avenorians speak of the city as it was a generation ago and do not entirely recognize it now.

For now, Avenor remains mostly what it was, but rules are subtly being changed, entering the city is harder, fewer students are attending the college, and the citizens are more weary of the future than before.
