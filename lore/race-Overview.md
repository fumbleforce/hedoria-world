The following are races, make each race its own trait, and put the race as a trait group. Make home regions for each race, named with one or two words.

  

Humans. The pre-Ensoulment majority race, reduced by the Wartide and the post-war settlement to four peripheral kingdoms: Avenor on the south coast, Udorath on the western Whitestone, Nadorim in the lake-country, and Kelmar in the northern highlands. Median build; regional variation in coloring; lifespan around seventy years. Worship the Hand Above as a folk-pervasive cosmic principle, institutionally light. The Brotherhood of Wizards at Telinor functions as the faith's institutional face: agoge-trained warrior-mystics, antagonistic to the witch line. Outside Avenor, witchcraft is illegal and the Quelled are farmed as livestock; Avenor alone outlawed the trade and protects witches and refugees under the queen's writ. The three non-Avenor kingdoms are allied against Avenor; the third soul-war is brewing.

  
  

Hofnar. Equine-humanoid; tall, broad-shouldered, mane-haired, hoof-toed, slight muzzle, some lineages with small temple horns. Males barrel-chested and heavy; females slimmer and faster. Lifted from steppe herds. Live in painted clan-wagon-cities that migrate seasonally — no fixed capitals. Organized by clan, confederated into seven Riderings led by elder councils. The continent's overland traders: neutral by reputation, scrupulously contract-bound, indispensable to everyone, allied with no one. Reliability is identity; a broken contract destroys standing for generations. Participate in the Quelled trade, which is splitting progressive and traditional clans toward an open break.

  
  

Draklid. Drake-descended; tall, lean, sinewy, digitigrade, long active tail, scales in rust/copper/ochre/bronze (rare black), clan-marked along the flanks. Long narrow muzzle, slit-pupil amber/yellow/red eyes, sweeping fan of horns, hooked claws, small vestigial wings useless for flight but expressive in display and adorned in ceremony. Live in cliff-cut sandstone clan-holds in the high desert and broken plateau. Each clan is sovereign; never been unified, openly hostile to the suggestion. Long-lived (matriarchs into their fifth century). Patient, calculating, formally polite, scrupulously fair, terrifying to cheat. Younger sons travel as mercenaries, bodyguards, and oath-bound retainers. Dress in layered desert silks, gold at throat and wrists, clan sigils at the belt.

  
  

Cephalen. Cephalopod-descended; humanoid silhouette but soft and near-boneless, can squeeze through gaps a third their size. Pearl-and-slate rubbery skin that involuntarily shifts color with emotion. Slit-pupil eyes, webbed long-fingered hands, scalp-tentacles in place of hair (semi-prehensile, used for fine work). Amphibious. Live in half-submerged coastal cities of mother-of-pearl, coral, and worked shell, with flooded lower floors and canal streets. Color-control is the central cultural discipline: children are transparent, adults hold steady, masters project false emotional states with precision. Took the seas by blockade two centuries ago after the human admiralties tried to subordinate them; control all open-water trade by treaty since. Sly as a Cephalen and Cephalen-honest are continental idioms. They do not deny the reputation.

  
  

Quelled. Youngest race, lifted from the domesticated livestock of pre-Wartide human farms. Several lineages: pig-quell (broad, snouted, bristled), cow-quell (tall, horned, long-faced), sheep-quell (small, fleece-haired, soft-faced), fowl-quell (lean, sharp, feather-down). Strongly animal in feature; not all stand fully upright. Crude tongues, no writing, no shared history beyond a few generations, no civilization. Bred, slaughtered, and worked as livestock and labor across the human kingdoms outside Avenor — the agricultural foundation of human society there. Avenor outlawed the trade two generations ago and grants escapees citizenship under the queen's writ; this is one of the chief reasons the rest of human Hedoria considers Avenor a traitor realm. Free Quelled elsewhere are isolated individuals — rare, hidden, short-lived.

  
  

Molvar. Tuber-and-root people; low-slung quadrupedal body with two stubby root-fingered upper arms, dense fibrous flesh under thick rind-skin in browns/ochres/umbers, pig-and-mole face with bright forward eyes and whisker-tendrils. Short-lived (~60 years). Live in brightly-lit tunnel-networks under low earthen mounds in central meadows and forest margins. No ruling class — neighborhood councils, decisions made over long meals. Hedonists: eat, drink, brew, garden, couple prolifically; plumpness admired, fatness more so. Genderless — any two can couple, either or both can produce a spud (planted in family soil, sprouts a child after half a year). Speak to roots and plants — coax tunnels into shape, persuade crops to thrive; sometimes hired by surface farmers. Not warlike, but devastating in tunnel defense — broke human armies in the First Soul-War and have been left alone since. Inter-network defensive pact is unwritten and absolute.

  
  

Threshi. Locust-mantis people, chitin-plated in dust-browns/tans/ochres/sand-yellows. Tall and narrow-bodied, triangular head with compound eyes and folding mandibles, segmented wing-panels along the back (short flights, long glides). Wartide race that held its ground when the working caught it. Strongly dimorphic: females are larger, broader, with chitin shovel-forearms (plowing, sowing, egg-laying — clutches of 20–40 several times yearly); males are leaner with folding sickle-blade forearms (harvest and war — same instrument). High mortality, faster replacement, surplus of unattached males every season. Aristocratic, ruled by warrior-king from a central capital; succession by challenge or claim, no king dies peacefully. Closed to outside trade. After every harvest, landless males ride out to take land, grain, and Quelled from the human borders — three centuries of inches has put significant former human territory under Threshi grain. Periodic field-worker risings, brutally suppressed.

  
  

Fernwarg. Wolf-and-fern people of the central forests. Tall, lean, upright, digitigrade, clawed hands and feet. Wolf-derived face — long muzzle, mobile ears, amber/yellow eyes, full canid expression-register. Coat is fur grown in fern-frond layers along back/shoulders/arms/tail, deep green to forest-dark, seasonally shifting; perfect camouflage in undergrowth. Spinal mane lifts in display, woven with bones, beads, and trophies — its size reads standing. Matriarchal by anatomy: females are larger, heavier, more muscular, with denser manes; pack-mothers carry headdresses of layered fronds. First birth is dangerous and the central passage of a female's life. Females choose mates. Males cannot lead their birth-pack — the most ambitious never will — so the ambitious leave and serve abroad as mercenaries, scouts, retainers. Packs are extended matrilines of 12–50, organized into forest-confederations. Share their forests with the Raknid by ancient unwritten compact (Fernwarg in open woodland, Raknid in deep old-growth). Two voices: long-carrying howls between packs, and an evening pack-laughter — a rising chittering chorus that disturbs human visitors. Locked in long political dispute with the bordering human kingdoms over forest rights; pressure building toward the third soul-war.

  

Raknid. Redeemed wartide race — the brood-line that turned during the Ensoulment and fought the rest of the Wartide alongside Avenoria's allies. Spider-derived: tall, slender, pale chitin-plated bodies in ivory, bone-white, soft grey, or glossy black. Two human-set arms plus two long jointed clawed spider-legs descending from the lower body — walk in a four-beat gait. Six eyes (large humanlike upper pair, four smaller lower), small folding chelicerae at the mouth, scalp grows a webbed mantle of pale silken fibers. Soft clicking voices, long-lived (comparable to Draklid). No homeland — their parent-race's high country was scoured and remains uninhabited. Live in scattered small communities of dozens to a few hundred in marginal hill country and forest edges. Most Hedorians do not distinguish them from what they came from: refused service, charged double, run out of towns at dusk. Communities are routinely burned out by neighboring lords or witch-hunters, and they rebuild elsewhere — they teach their children against returning violence. Take patient work: scholars, archivists, healers, translators, the occasional witch. Avenor accepts them as full citizens; the Queen's College has trained Raknid witches for over a century, who serve the realm with exacting practice and fierce loyalty. Dress simply in muted earth-tones — undyed linens, soft leathers, layered scarves and travel-cloaks — sometimes ornamented with small objects woven from their own silk.

  


  
  

Vorok. Wartide-bred labor race, left ownerless when Avenoria's working caught them mid-labor; reorganized into trading consortiums. No women — reproduction is contractual through Quelled and other women hired or bought as bearers. Tall, heavy-jawed with prominent lower tusks, broad brows, large folding ears; skin in dull tones (grey, slate, smoky blue, dusty orange) varying without pattern between siblings; body adapts to lifelong work, slab-shouldered laborers and lean foremen produced by the same lineage. The continent's miners and prospecting geologists; the homeland is largely mined out and active operations run on foreign ground under contract. Organized through seven great houses with no kings or councils; principals buy share-blocks. Crude, transactional, paid by the hour. Free crews drift into banditry; exiled Voroks take work in the wider world.

  

Primes. The elders of Hedoria — already a people before the Ensoulment, with histories that run further back than any other race's. Tall, heavy-built, bear-bodied and broad-shouldered, dense white fur thickening at chest, neck, and tail. Pale chimpanzee-like face with a long lion muzzle, long furry tail used for balance and gesture, dexterous five-fingered hands, broad padded snow-feet. Robe in deep blue, ochre, vermilion, and gold, layered and embroidered with clan, monastery, and lineage markings — small bone relics (finger-bones, teeth, jaw fragments bound in worked metal) worn at throat, chest, wrists, and belt. Live in great cliff-cut monasteries terraced into the base of cold mountains, hung with banners and skull-iconography, with bone-vaults cut deep into the rock. Seven great monasteries currently active; lost vaults from the Unmade era are recovered across generations. Practice ancestor-magic: a slow, exact, unflashy art of invoking specific named dead by rite and offering, drawing on chained lineages of ancestors back across millennia. Excellent at knowledge, healing, divination, true-naming, untangling curses, and long-time work — useless for battle-magic. Power is anchored: a Prime at home with full vault-access is enormously powerful; a Prime on the road, carrying only three or four portable relics (named ancestors in their own right), is a fraction of that. The wizard colleges fear them quietly — Primes hold the actual historical record the colleges have spent centuries rewriting. Black-market grave-robbing of Prime bones exists; the magic does not work for thieves, and the families involved suffer untraceable misfortunes across generations. Did not fight the first Wartide alongside Avenoria; the bone-vaults that made that decision still hold the magic, still counsel preservation, and still resist young Primes who want to commit to the second. Long-standing peerage with Avenor has gone quiet under Sephilia's reordering. The Primes will warn, document, and watch. Their monasteries will survive whatever comes.
