### **What the Quelled actually are**

The Quelled are a young race in the deepest sense. Centuries old as a people, where Molvar and Metalites have at least the same span and Primes have *millennia*. The ensoulment lifted them as it lifted everyone, but it lifted them *whole* — not from beast-precursors with their own deep proto-cultures, but from ordinary unensouled animals with no inherited civilization to draw on. Pigs were pigs. Cows were cows. They had social structures, yes — herds, bonds, simple hierarchies — but nothing that translates to language, art, history, governance, theology. When they were ensouled, they woke into full minds with **nothing to think with**.

A Molvar child is born into millennia of song and story and craft. A Metalite child inherits a civilization of forge-cities and metalwork going back further than human kingdoms have records. A Quell child, in the wild — in the small free villages that still exist in the deep wilds and the protected groves — is born into a culture *centuries old at most*, built from scratch, fragmentary, struggling to develop language complex enough to express what their minds can hold. Quelled languages exist but are crude — small vocabularies, simple grammar, lots of gesture and song-cry to fill in what the words can't carry. Their oldest poems are barely poems by Molvar standards. Their theology is half-formed. Their histories don't reach back to the ensoulment because no one was writing anything down, and oral tradition for a four-or-five-generation race produces only so much.

But the **minds are there**. A Quell raised among Molvar from birth grows up speaking Molvar fluently, reading Molvar literature, understanding Molvar philosophy — and can, by adulthood, contribute to it. A Quell raised in an Avenorian noble household becomes literate in three languages, plays an instrument well, holds her own in court conversation. The capability is intact. What's missing is *cultural inheritance*, and cultural inheritance can be supplied by whoever raises the child.

This is the horror.

### **What humans actually have, then**

Most humans are not, in fact, raising sub-human animals. They are **raising people from infancy in conditions designed to ensure those people never develop**. They are taking children with full minds and giving them only enough language to follow simple commands, only enough socialization to function in a barn or a field or a kitchen. The dullness of a chattel-Quell adult is not the dullness of a Quell — it is the dullness of any person raised in utter cultural and intellectual deprivation. The chattel trade isn't farming animals. It's *manufacturing* the ignorance that justifies the trade.

Some humans know this. Most don't. The ones who do mostly don't say so. The ones who say so mostly don't say it loudly.

The handful of Quelled who escape, or who are bought out by abolitionist patrons and properly raised, are *living refutations* of the entire system. A literate, articulate former chattel-Quell speaking in court is the kind of figure who, in another world, ends slaveries. In this world, she gets attacked and her existence dismissed as a freak — *that one's special, the rest are still beasts, you can't generalize from a single trained example, etc.* The southern human kingdoms have laws making it illegal to publicly display educated Quelled, framed as protecting the public from "unnatural confusion." This is exactly as ugly as it sounds.

### **The free Quelled**

Outside the chattel kingdoms, free Quelled communities exist. They are mostly small, mostly hidden or sheltered:

  - **Wild villages** in the deep groves and forest fringes, often under Molvar protection. These are where the native Quell cultures are slowly being built — the languages, the songs, the handful of myths, the first written scripts (where they have writing at all). A traveler who finds a free pig-folk village in a Molvar forest and is welcomed in finds something fragile and beautiful — a culture being invented in real time by people who know they have no inherited one.
  - **Avenorian Quell** — full citizens of Avenor since the abolition two generations ago. Most are still recovering from generational trauma; the first generation born free is now reaching adulthood, and they are *something*. They are scholars, artisans, soldiers, witches. Avenorian Quell are politically central to the realm's identity now: their existence is Avenor's loudest statement to the rest of the human world. They are also a magnet for kidnapping by southern slaver-traders, who can sell a properly-trained Quell for a fortune to decadent collectors.
  - **Quell among the Raknid, the Hofnar, and others** — varies. Hofnar complicate the picture (they trade in Quelled and they shelter Quelled escapees, depending on the family). The Raknid, given their own history, treat free Quelled with fierce protectiveness; a chattel caravan that crosses a Raknid hold's territory is asking for trouble.

The free Quelled are still building everything — language, story, religion, history. A Quell scholar in Avenor working on the **first formal grammar** of one of the Quell languages, or compiling the **first written collection** of pig-folk oral songs, is doing the kind of work that defines a civilization for the next thousand years. This is a setting where it is genuinely *the early years of a people*, with all the urgency and possibility that carries.

### **What this means for the world's moral architecture**

The chattel trade is no longer "humans farming animals that happen to be ensouled." It is **humans enslaving people and culturally lobotomizing them to maintain the fiction that they were animals to begin with**. This is more horrible, not less, and it's the right level of horrible for the adult-toned world you want.

It also changes what abolition *means*. Avenor didn't free animals. Avenor freed *people*, and is now bearing the long civilizational cost of having a population suddenly thrust into full citizenship without the inherited tools to navigate it. The first generations of free Quelled in Avenor have produced enormous cultural ferment — heroic adaptation, terrible failures, criminal sub-cultures forming in the gap, brilliant scholars emerging from nothing. Avenor's experience of post-abolition society is the most interesting social experiment in Hedoria, and it's still in progress.

It changes what **Quell-rights advocacy** looks like in the human kingdoms. There are humans — small numbers, mostly clergy of certain heretical sects, a few intellectuals, certain merchants who have seen things they cannot un-see — who quietly oppose the trade. Their argument is not that the Quelled are "actually intelligent, look at the Avenorian examples"; the southern kingdoms have a counter-argument ready for that. Their argument, where they make it, is closer to the Avenorian theological position: *the working ensouled them, the working does not lie, what we have made of them is a sin against the working itself*. This is religious dissent, and it gets people in trouble.

It changes what **ordinary humans believe**. Most humans, asked directly, would say something like: *they're animals, look at them, they don't even talk properly*. They have not asked themselves why the Quelled don't talk properly. They have been told the Quelled don't talk properly because the Quelled are stupid, and the Quelled they have personally seen — chattel-raised in barns since infancy — visibly cannot talk properly, which seems to confirm it. The system is self-justifying as long as no one looks closely. Most humans don't look closely.

It changes what a **Quell PC** can be. A Quell PC is no longer "a sympathetic but limited NPC type." A Quell PC is *anything a player wants to play* — Avenorian witch, Molvar-raised forester, escaped slave finding her voice, free-village scholar building her people's first written canon, vengeful escapee burning her way back to her birth-farm to free her siblings. The role is dramatically rich. The character will be encountered by humans as a slave-class creature even when she's a witch-knight in full regalia, and the friction this produces is constant story material.

### **The drover question, refined**

Hofnar trading in Quelled is now genuinely darker, because they are trading in people. Most Hofnar genuinely don't think of it that way — the Hofnar position has historically tracked the dominant human view, and most Hofnar tell themselves the Quelled are simple creatures who do best with caretakers, etc. But Hofnar are in regular contact with Avenorian Quelled, with free-village Quelled, with the rare escaped Quell who finds them on the road, and the cognitive dissonance is *getting worse* generation by generation.

This is one of the slow-burning moral movements in the world: **Hofnar society is starting to fracture on the chattel question**. The progressive Hofnar families increasingly refuse the contracts. The traditional Hofnar families increasingly defend the trade as economic necessity. The split runs through clans, through trade-alliances, sometimes through individual marriages. A Hofnar PC's family position on the chattel trade is one of the most important things about them.

### **Updated picture**

**Core four**: Humans, Molvar (with Rootlings), Metalites (with Metlings), Hofnar (with wild herd-things). **Significant minors**: Primes, Fernwarg, \[placeholder — greenskin\]. **The Quelled**: the youngest race, fully ensouled and fully capable, raised as chattel by most humans through deliberate cultural deprivation. Free communities exist in Avenor, Molvar lands, Raknid holds, and elsewhere, building their cultures from scratch. The chattel trade is the world's central moral atrocity.
