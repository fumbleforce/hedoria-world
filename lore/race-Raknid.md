# The Raknid

The Raknid are a redeemed wartide race — the brood-line that turned during Avenoria's working, broke from its kin, and fought the rest of the Wartide alongside Avenoria's allies. They emerged as people and have lived as people in scattered small communities since. Most of Hedoria still does not distinguish them from what they came from.

## **Origin and Form**

The Raknid descend from one of the spider-races of the Wartide, a brood-people of the high broken country. When Avenoria's working passed through the host, most of it did not break. One brood-line did — perhaps a sixth of the whole — and turned on its kin in the same hour, fighting the rest of the host through the campaigns that followed. They were called the Raknid even then, originally as a slur, later as their own name.

  

They emerged in the bodies they had been wearing, reshaped only as far as the soul required. An adult Raknid stands taller than a human, lightly built, with long limbs and a slight frame. Their skin is pale chitin, plated along the torso and limbs in articulated segments, smooth and faintly cool to the touch. Beneath the human-set arms, two further legs descend from the lower body — long, jointed, ending in fine clawed points — so that a Raknid walks on four spidery legs in a four-beat gait.

  

Their faces have six eyes set in two arcs — an upper pair large and humanlike, four lower eyes smaller and clustered. The mouth carries a pair of small fanged chelicerae, folded close to the lips when not in use. From the scalp grows a fine webbed mantle of pale silken fibers, half hair and half spun thread. Some lineages retain a vestigial spinneret at the base of the spine, useless for any practical purpose but occasionally worked into small ritual objects.

  

Their voices are soft and slightly clicking. Their lifespans are long, comparable to the Draklid.

## **Life in the World**

The Raknid have no homeland. The high country where the parent race ran was scoured after the Wartide and is uninhabited still. They live in scattered small communities of a few dozen to a few hundred — quiet settlements in the hill country between human realms, on the margins of the great forests, in back valleys other peoples have not bothered to take. They farm, craft, and build modestly.

  

Most Raknid communities have been burned out at least once in living memory. A village lives quietly for a generation, then a neighboring lord's child sickens, or a witch-hunter passes through, and the community is destroyed. The survivors scatter and rebuild somewhere else. The Raknid do not fight back; they teach their children against returning violence under any circumstance short of immediate physical defense.

  

The redemption is acknowledged in formal contexts — Avenorian law recognizes it, the older post-Wartide treaties reference it — but in ordinary speech most of Hedoria collapses the distinction. *Spider-people*, *broodkin*, *the wartide-blood*. A Raknid traveling alone through human country can expect to be refused service at most inns, to pay double the posted price, and to be followed out of any town they enter at dusk.

## **Vocations**

The Raknid take work that rewards patience and care. Many become scholars — historians, archivists, copyists, linguists. Many become healers. A handful work as translators and diplomats. A small number become witches.

  

Avenor is the exception. The realm accepted Raknid refugees in the years after the Wartide and grants them full standing under the queen's writ. Perhaps a few thousand live within the city's walls. The Queen's College accepts Raknid students and has done so for over a century. The Raknid witches it produces are a small cohort, never more than a few dozen at any time, with a reputation for exacting practice and fierce loyalty to the realm.
