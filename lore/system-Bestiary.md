# Hedoria Bestiary

A reference document for the creatures of Hedoria — the wildlife, vermin, beasts, trolls, demonic remnants, magical beasts, and greater monsters that range the continent outside the bounds of the ensouled races. Race entries belong in separate documents.

  

The bestiary is organized in seven tiers, broadly by threat and rarity. The early tiers are the everyday hazards of travel and the back-country grind. The later tiers are the rare encounters and the boss-class quarry that take a party and a plan.

## **Tier 1 — Common wildlife**

**Auroch** — Wild unensouled cattle of the Deep Steppes and Long Steppe. Bull-shouldered, horn-spread the width of a wagon. Solitary bulls common; small breeding groups graze the open. Charge when threatened.

  

**Tuskboar** — Foul-tempered plague-carrying boar of the Reach margins, Whitestone fens, and Avenor backwoods. Solitary or sow-with-young. Charges on sight.

  

**Crag-cat** — Lynx of the Shieldcaps, Hinderance Highlands, and Impasse heights. Solitary daylight hunter. Stalks across the scree and commits in a single leap.

  

**Rhok** — Flightless ostrich-cousin of the Long Steppe and Sun-Scoured canyon floors. Tall as a man, kicks with a single splitting claw, runs faster than a horse over distance. Solitary or in pairs. Aggressive in the rutting season, indifferent the rest.

  

**Wisent** — Forest bison of the Reach river-valleys and meadow-corridors. Solitary bulls and small breeding groups. Charges if approached past a known distance.

## **Tier 2 — Vermin**

**Throatworm** — Knee-thick burrower of the loam. Erupts from beneath at close range and fights rather than retreats. Mound-and-disturbance signs mark territory. Reach margins, Long Steppe edges, Avenor pasture country.

  

**Husk** — Once human. Survivors of Wartide attacks where something drained them and let the body walk. Pale, bent, milky-eyed, mouths working without words. What was a person is gone. Reduced to primal instincts — hunger, fear, the urge to return to a place that mattered. Wander the back-country of every region near old battlefields and Hollows-fringes, often drifting back toward the homes they once lived in. Solo, pitiful, dangerous. Bounty paid in most kingdoms.

  

**Gristlemaw** — Knee-high, scaly grey, heavily built around an oversized jaw. Lurks in barrows and farmstead cellars, takes one or two big meals a season. Found alone or with a single mate. Will break a kneecap and drag.

  

**Pinker** — Hairless pale primate the size of a large dog, walks knuckled, climbs walls. One or two haunt a ruined building or back-alley cellar. Pries with long fingers, breaks shutters, takes infants and small livestock. Patient enough to scout a household before striking, clever enough to use simple tools.

## **Tier 3 — Hazardous beasts**

**Drakehound** — Wartide-bred war-dog. Waist-high, scaled along spine and flanks, long-jawed. Mute pack-hunter — three to five, no baying. Found across the back-country of every region.

  

**Cliff-wyrm** — Limbless serpent of the Sun-Scoured Plateau and Shieldcap upper ranges. Length of three wagons. Suns on flat rock in late afternoon. Constricts, swallows.

  

**Mawpig** — Wartide-bred carrion-beast. Squat tusked thing the size of a calf, mouth that splits the skull horizontally. Solo or paired across wild back-country. Charges, doesn't ambush.

  

**Striglaw** — Tall thin biped, twin pairs of arms — upper pair gripping, lower pair barbed. Wartide-bred, now solitary wanderers. Reach back-country, Impasse, Hollows fringes. Picks fights with anything it crosses.

  

**Ulk** — Slime, dog-sized to bull-sized depending on age, semi-firm like very soft rubber, semi-transparent amber. Cuts under a blade rather than parts around it. Engulfs prey of any gender to implant — ulk reproduce by hosting their seed-pearls inside larger animals, who pass them later. Found in old galleries, ruin basements, Echoing Hollow, abandoned Whitestone mines. Slow but committed in pursuit.

  

**Slogger** — Aggressive slug the size of a calf, marsh edges of the Whitestone, Lake Wildering shallows, Reach bog-pools. Acid mucus strips contact, blunt charge breaks bone. Wide silver track through reed and mud marks its passage.

  

**Brood** — Cart-sized spider-thing, abdomen heavy with clutch. Lairs in deep Reach old-growth, the cave systems of the Shieldcap foothills and the Impasse, abandoned galleries, and Hollows fringes. Bite paralyzes; ovipositor implants regardless of host's gender, into mouth, throat, or below. Hosts mostly survive. The Brood itself a real fight: heavy chitin, eight legs, abdomen vulnerable from below. Larders contain living people who can be freed.

  

**Hookwight** — Long pallid serpent, the length of two men, jaw hinged to swallow what its head can clear. Grassland and meadow country. Strikes from cover but commits and fights through. Venom causes muscle slackness.

  

**Beetlebull** — Ox-sized horned beetle of the Reach margins, Long Steppe edges, and Sun-Scoured canyon floors. Solitary or paired. Charges with a single great horn, plates the back against pursuit. Slow to turn.

## **Tier 4 — Trolls & ogres**

**Mauler** — Ogre. Twelve feet tall, slab-shouldered, club-armed, lone keeper of a roadside or pass. Builds itself a rough den and leaves bones outside. Found on overland routes across the Reach, the Hinderance, and the Impasse. Slow, cunning enough to set a simple ambush of fallen logs and dropped stones. Takes captives back to the den for use over days.

  

**Bridge-troll** — Squat broad troll of river-crossings and old culverts. Lake Wildering tributaries, Avenor backcountry, Reach river-valleys. Lairs under stone bridges and demands toll in food, livestock, or person. Speaks basic common-tongue, enough to name a price and curse a refusal. Will let payers pass. Will not let non-payers pass. Skin grows back from wounds slowly but not from fire.

  

**Skarn** — High-country troll of the Shieldcap upper passes and the Sun-Scoured plateau heights. Stone-skinned, two heads, the second mostly silent. Hunts goats and travelers. Lairs in glacier hollows and slot-canyon overhangs. Slow on level ground, terrifying on broken ground.

## **Tier 5 — Demonic remnants**

The demonic remnants are intelligent in the full sense — they speak any tongue they've heard, follow an argument, weigh an offer, and remember a promise. They are also nasty by inclination, drawn by appetite and cruelty in ways the ensouled races find foreign. A traveler with the wit to talk one down, change its terms, or buy it off can sometimes walk away. Most travelers do not have the wit. The demonic register is a conversation, but it is not a friendly one, and the conversation goes badly more often than well.

  

**Skellach** — Oxen-legged horned humanoid, tall as a man, knot-bearded, walks woodland clearings and ruin courtyards in the Reach and Avenor backwoods. Hunts alone or in pairs. Eats fruit and keeps an orchard-camp. Fights with a heavy wood stave, longer than a man, used two-handed in a wide-arc style. Speaks well, with a slow rural cadence, and enjoys conversation when not provoked. Takes captives of either sex back to the orchard and binds them with woven vine — a particular fetish for the binding itself, and for letting other creatures of the wood take their turn with the bound. Releases them after, into the forest, dazed but living. Will sometimes accept a basket of good fruit and a length of cord in trade for not pressing the matter further.

  

**Imp** — Knee-high demonic remnant. Red or ash-grey skin, vestigial wings that don't carry weight, prehensile tail, sharp teeth. Solo encounters in ruins, cellars, and Hollows-fringe back-rooms. Small fire-magic — a thrown handful of flame, a burning palm-print on flesh — and a cunning mean enough to be a real fight in a closed room. Talkative, and most imps will negotiate before committing to violence. Their offers are clever, their terms unfavorable, and the small print binds.

  

**Succor** — Female-form demonic remnant, beautiful in a wrong way the mind smooths over until too late. Walks the road in plain sight near Hollows-fringes and old battlefields. Speaks with a warmth that is its first weapon. Charms by voice and touch, drains the charmed over a single night, leaves a Husk. Will fight directly when the charm fails — claws extend, the mouth opens wider than it should. A traveler who refuses the charm cleanly can sometimes parley; a succor that has been refused twice in the same season can be bargained with at unusually fair terms, by her standards.

  

**Goret** — Imp's larger cousin. Waist-high, thickset, broken horns, drools coal-smoke. Carries a crude iron weapon and uses it competently. Speaks in a low rasping common-tongue, swears constantly, prefers haggling to fighting when the haggling pays better. Dens in pairs in old foundries, abandoned galleries, and Hollows-fringe ruins. Will fight to the death over a hoard.

## **Tier 6 — Magical beasts**

The magical beasts are intelligent in a quieter way than the demonic remnants — not language-using, but aware. They watch. They remember. They make decisions a wild beast does not. A glasshorn knows the difference between a hunter and a witness, and a hearthcat chooses who to lead and where to. The magical register rewards patience and punishes greed in ways the kingdoms have learned by long bad experience and quietly continue to ignore.

  

**Glimmercat** — House-cat-sized, opalescent fur, eyes that hold a slow inner light. Solitary in open country across the continent. Skittish but curious.

  

**Lumefox** — Red-fox-sized, coat shifting between russet and silver depending on the angle of light, brush-tail tipped white. Solitary in meadows, hedgerows, and ruin-margins across the continent. Common enough to grind, valuable enough to bother. The bulk of the licensed Avenorian witch-trade is lumefox. Trapping is a profession in three kingdoms.

  

**Lampfowl** — Pheasant-sized ground bird of the Reach edges and Whitestone uplands. Tail feathers glow soft yellow at dusk. Flightless, runs and fights.

  

**Sylvan** — Male humanoid magical creature, full-grown the size of a slight man, skin pearl or moss-green, found alone near old wells, deep pools, and sacred springs in the Reach and Avenor foothills. Beautiful in a still alien way, mute but watchful, fights with a small bone knife and minor water-magic. Mana-rich in the way a man is mana-rich, and far more so. Captured alive sells for many times the kill-price; the witch-academies want them for filling work, and a sylvan in good condition can fill a senior witch in a single session — the highest-quality donor source short of a Glasshorn. Officially forbidden in most kingdoms. Licensed in most kingdoms.

  

**Naia** — Female counterpart to the sylvan, found in the same waters and groves. Beautiful, mute, fights with a bone knife and minor water-magic. Cannot fill a witch — but a witch with a stocked larder of naia parts can extend a single filling much further than otherwise. The kill-trade hunts them; the living-trade does not bother.

  

**Stagling** — Fawn-sized horned creature of the Reach meadow-corridors. Antlers carry visible mana-glow at first light. Runs but does not flee far. A common starter quarry for mana-hunters. Antlers grow back annually if the creature is spared, and the higher-grade trade is a managed harvest.

  

**Pyrelynx** — Cat the size of a wolf, coat the color of banked coals, warm to the touch even in winter. Sun-Scoured Plateau and southern Shieldcap foothills. Solitary, ambushes once and fights through.

## **Tier 7 — Greater monsters**

The greater monsters are intelligent and wicked. They speak, they plan, they remember grudges across years. They are not negotiable in the manner of the demonic remnants — what they want is rarely something an ordinary traveler can offer, and what they offer in exchange is rarely something an ordinary traveler should accept. A bloodthane knows the names of the villages it has emptied. A centaur-thane knows the breeding stock in its back-camp by face. They are not beasts. They are why the back-country stays empty.

  

**Maulerbear** — Wartide-bred bear, twice the size of a true bear, shaggy and scarred, jaw plated with bony ridges. Solitary, claims a stretch of forest or broken country and walks it openly. Reach interior, Shieldcap foothills, Hinderance back-country. Will pursue across territories. Speaks little, but speaks — a slow rumbling common-tongue, used mostly to taunt and to name what it intends to do.

  

**Bloodthane** — Wartide-officer remnant. Tall, chitin-plated, intelligent, command-class. Lairs in fortified ruins on the Hollows fringes, walks the parapet of its claimed ground. Drains humans to Husks by long touch. Speaks fluently in any tongue it has heard, holds court in its ruin in the manner of the lord it once was, and will receive emissaries who arrive correctly. Most emissaries do not arrive correctly. Commands lesser Wartide things in its territory.

  

**Kelker** — Twelve feet tall, two stilt-legs, crown of arms around a central feeding-orifice. Strides across broken country at impossible speed. Sun-Scoured Plateau, Impasse, Hollows fringes. Speaks rarely, in a voice that comes from the feeding-orifice and is not pleasant to hear. What it says is usually a question, and the questions are old.

  

**Loam-Drinker** — Vast burrowing worm of deep tunnels and worked-out homeland country. Surfaces in old pit-works to feed. Speaks through the ground itself when it chooses to — a low resonant common-tongue felt in the chest before it is heard in the ear. The conversations are rare and remembered. A confirmed kill is a generational event.

  

**Centaur-thane** — Wartide-bred centauroid. Horse-bodied, humanoid torso, bull-thick. Roams the back-country of the Long Steppe and Reach margins in bands of three to five led by a thane. Speaks well, with the formality of an old soldiery, and the thane will parley before a fight if the parley promises better return than the fight. Takes captives of either sex on raids and breeds them in a back-camp for stock. Bands fightable on the open ground; the thane is the boss kill, armored, heavy axe, war-cry that staggers.
